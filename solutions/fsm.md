# SAP Field Service Management (FSM)

## Solution Overview
SAP Field Service Management (FSM) is SAP's cloud-based solution for companies that dispatch technicians, engineers, or service crews to customer sites. It manages the full field service lifecycle: service request intake, intelligent scheduling and dispatching, technician mobile execution (with offline capability), parts and inventory management for field teams, and customer self-service for booking and tracking. Integrated with SAP Cloud ERP for service orders, billing, and spare parts, FSM closes the loop between what happens in the field and what gets invoiced. It is the right choice for organisations with 50 or more field technicians where scheduling efficiency, first-time-fix rates, and technician utilisation are key business drivers.

## Key Capabilities

### Scheduling and Dispatching
- AI-powered scheduling optimisation — automatically assigns the right technician based on skills, location, availability, and parts on hand
- Drag-and-drop dispatch board for planners — visual map and Gantt view of all open and assigned work
- Dynamic re-scheduling — real-time response to cancellations, emergencies, or traffic delays
- Crowd-sourced and contractor workforce management — schedule third-party technicians alongside internal teams
- SLA-aware scheduling — prioritise work orders based on contract terms and response-time commitments

### Technician Mobile App
- Offline-capable iOS/Android app — works in the field without connectivity, syncs when back online
- Work order details, service history, and customer asset information at the technician's fingertips
- Step-by-step guided troubleshooting and digital work instructions
- Time and materials capture at point of service — no paperwork to submit later
- Photo, video, and signature capture for job completion evidence
- Parts request and consumption recording in the field

### Customer Engagement
- Customer self-service portal — book service appointments, track technician location, view service history
- Automated customer notifications — appointment confirmations, technician on-the-way alerts, completion summaries
- Customer satisfaction surveys triggered automatically after service completion
- Service contract and warranty management — customer-facing visibility into entitlements

### Parts and Inventory
- Field technician trunk stock management — know what each technician has on their van
- Parts reservation and transfer for planned work orders
- Integration with SAP Cloud ERP procurement for parts replenishment
- Return merchandise authorisation (RMA) for defective parts

### Service Analytics
- First-time-fix rate (FTFR) tracking by technician, region, and asset type
- Technician utilisation and travel time efficiency dashboards
- Mean time to repair (MTTR) and SLA compliance reporting
- Revenue per work order and parts margin analysis

## Business Value Propositions
- **Higher first-time-fix rates**: 10–20% improvement in FTFR through skills-based dispatching, pre-loaded service history, and guided troubleshooting — reducing costly return visits
- **Technician utilisation**: 15–25% improvement in billable hours through AI-optimised scheduling and reduced windshield time
- **Customer satisfaction**: Real-time tracking, appointment confirmations, and faster resolution improve NPS and renewal rates
- **Faster billing**: Parts and time captured at point of service flow directly to ERP for immediate invoicing — eliminates billing lag of days or weeks
- **Reduced admin burden**: Paperless field execution eliminates paperwork, manual timesheet entry, and back-office data re-entry
- **SLA compliance**: AI scheduling ensures response-time commitments are met and exceptions are flagged before SLA breach
- **Connected service lifecycle**: From customer call through dispatch, execution, parts, and billing — one connected process vs. four disconnected systems

## Pain Points Addressed
- Schedulers spending hours every morning manually matching technicians to work orders based on spreadsheets and phone calls
- High rate of repeat visits because the first technician didn't have the right part or the right skills
- Technicians calling back to the office constantly to get service history, part numbers, or customer information
- Billing cycle takes 2–4 weeks after service completion because paperwork has to come back and be re-entered
- No real-time visibility into where technicians are or what the status of open work orders is
- SLA breach happening before anyone realises there's a problem — discovered when the customer calls to complain
- Parts management for field teams is chaotic — no visibility into van stock, constant shortages and excess
- Customer satisfaction is low because of long wait times, no communication, and repeat visits
- Contractors and third-party technicians scheduled in a completely separate system

## Discovery Angles — What to Probe
- **Scheduling process**: Walk me through how a service request turns into a scheduled appointment today — from when the call comes in to when a technician is on their way.
- **First-time-fix rate**: What percentage of work orders are completed on the first visit? When a second visit is needed, what's the most common reason?
- **Technician productivity**: What percentage of a technician's day is actually spent on customer-facing work vs. travel, admin, or waiting? Is that tracked?
- **Billing cycle**: How long after a service visit is complete does the invoice go to the customer? What causes that delay?
- **Parts management**: How do you manage parts for field technicians? How often does a visit fail because the technician didn't have the right part?
- **Customer experience**: What does the customer experience look like today — can they book online, track the technician, see their service history?
- **SLA management**: How do you monitor SLA compliance across your open work orders? How do you know when you're at risk of a breach before it happens?
- **Workforce mix**: Do you use a mix of employed technicians and contractors? How do you manage scheduling across both groups?
- **System landscape**: What systems are you using today for scheduling, dispatch, and work order management? What are the biggest gaps or frustrations?
