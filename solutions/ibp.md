# SAP Integrated Business Planning (IBP)

## Solution Overview
SAP Integrated Business Planning (IBP) is SAP's cloud-based supply chain planning platform, replacing SAP APO and integrating demand planning, supply planning, inventory optimisation, and S&OP into a single, real-time environment. Built on SAP HANA with a native SAP Analytics Cloud interface, IBP gives planners live visibility across the entire supply chain — from customer demand signals through to supplier capacity — enabling faster, better-informed decisions. It is the right choice for companies with complex, multi-tier supply chains where planning latency, siloed spreadsheets, and reactive firefighting are preventing growth and profitability.

## Key Capabilities

### IBP for Demand
- Statistical demand forecasting with 50+ models, auto-selected by SKU
- Demand sensing — use of near-real-time signals (POS, shipment, IoT) to improve short-horizon accuracy
- AI/ML-based consensus demand planning across commercial and operations teams
- Promotion and event planning with lift modelling
- Collaborative demand review with web-based customer input

### IBP for Supply (Supply and Response Planning)
- Multi-echelon supply planning across plants, DCs, and suppliers
- Constrained supply planning respecting capacity, lead time, and inventory limits
- Supply response — real-time allocation and response to supply disruptions
- Rough-cut capacity planning and capacity-constrained scheduling
- Integration with SAP Digital Manufacturing for shop floor feedback

### IBP for Sales and Operations (S&OP)
- Integrated S&OP process with structured review workflows (demand review, supply review, exec S&OP)
- What-if scenario modelling — compare multiple supply/demand scenarios in real time
- Financial integration — translate volume plan to revenue and margin plan
- KPI dashboards and exception-driven review for leadership teams

### IBP for Inventory
- Multi-echelon inventory optimisation (MEIO) — scientifically set safety stock across the network
- Service-level-driven inventory policies by SKU and location
- Working capital impact modelling — see the cash impact of inventory policy decisions
- Segmentation — ABC/XYZ analysis to drive different replenishment policies by segment

### Control Tower and Supply Chain Visibility
- End-to-end supply chain visibility from supplier to customer
- Exception management with root-cause analysis
- Integration with SAP Business Network for supplier order collaboration

## Business Value Propositions
- **Forecast accuracy improvement**: 10–20% reduction in forecast error through ML-powered demand sensing and statistical models
- **Inventory reduction**: 15–25% reduction in overall inventory while maintaining or improving service levels
- **Service level improvement**: Reduce stockouts and backorders by aligning supply with demand more accurately
- **Planning cycle acceleration**: Compress S&OP cycle from monthly to weekly; scenario planning in hours, not days
- **Working capital release**: Improved inventory positioning typically releases 10–20% of inventory value as cash
- **Supply chain resilience**: Faster response to disruptions through real-time visibility and scenario simulation
- **Replace APO**: Modern cloud platform replacing aging APO with lower TCO and continuous innovation

## Pain Points Addressed
- Monthly S&OP cycle that is already stale by the time it's published
- Demand planning done in spreadsheets — no consensus process, multiple versions of truth
- Inventory too high in the wrong places and too low where demand actually is
- No ability to quickly model the impact of a supply disruption or demand spike
- SAP APO approaching end of life with no cloud path
- Finance and supply chain running on different numbers — volume plan disconnected from financial plan
- Planners spending 80% of time gathering data, 20% on actual planning decisions
- Supplier lead times and capacities not visible until it's too late to react
- High expediting costs because supply response to demand signals is too slow

## Discovery Angles — What to Probe
- **S&OP maturity**: Walk me through your current S&OP process — how often does it run, who participates, and how long after month-end do you have a final plan?
- **Forecast accuracy**: What is your current forecast accuracy at the SKU/location level? How does a 10% forecast error manifest downstream?
- **Inventory profile**: Where do you typically hold most of your inventory? Do you have visibility into where it sits vs. where demand is?
- **Planning tools**: What tools are your planners using today — is it SAP APO, a third-party tool, spreadsheets? What are the biggest frustrations?
- **APO timeline**: If you're on SAP APO, have you had a conversation with SAP about the migration path to IBP?
- **Scenario planning**: The last time there was a major supply disruption — how long did it take to model the impact and get a recovery plan approved?
- **Finance alignment**: Does your supply chain volume plan connect to your financial forecast? Where does that handoff break down?
- **Supply disruptions**: In the past 12 months, what supply chain disruptions have you faced? What was the cost of the response?
- **Inventory vs. service level trade-off**: Is the pressure from the business to reduce inventory, improve service, or both? How do you currently balance that?

## Target Personas
- VP Supply Chain / Chief Supply Chain Officer: Wants resilience, service levels, and working capital improvement
- S&OP / IBP Process Owner: Wants an integrated planning process that connects commercial and operations
- Demand Planning Manager: Wants better forecast accuracy and less time in spreadsheets
- Inventory / Materials Manager: Wants to reduce excess stock while protecting service levels
- CFO / VP Finance: Wants working capital improvement and a connected volume-to-financial plan
- CIO / IT: Wants to replace aging APO infrastructure and move supply chain planning to cloud
- COO: Wants to make better, faster decisions when supply or demand conditions change
