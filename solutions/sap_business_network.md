# SAP Business Network — Solution Knowledge Base

## Solution Overview
SAP Business Network is the world's largest B2B trading network, connecting over 6 million businesses across procurement, supply chain, and logistics. It is the connectivity layer for SAP Ariba (source-to-pay), SAP Transportation Management, and broader supply chain collaboration. It enables seamless digital exchange of orders, confirmations, shipment notices, invoices, and freight documents between buyers, suppliers, and logistics providers — all on a single network.

## Key Capabilities

### Supplier Collaboration (Procurement Network)
- Electronic PO transmission and supplier acknowledgment
- Advance Shipment Notices (ASNs) with packing details
- Electronic invoicing with tax and compliance support in 70+ countries
- Catalog management for buyers' preferred supplier items
- Supply chain financing and early payment programs
- Supplier self-service portal for transaction status and discrepancy resolution

### Direct Materials Collaboration
- Purchase order collaboration with demand-supply matching
- Forecast sharing between buyer and supplier
- Component availability confirmation and exception management
- Just-in-time delivery scheduling
- Supplier-managed inventory (SMI) and consignment support
- Quality notification exchange

### Logistics Collaboration (Freight Network)
- Spot bidding and tendering to carrier network
- Load tender acceptance and rejection
- Electronic bill of lading and proof of delivery
- Real-time freight tracking across carriers and modes
- Freight invoice submission and automated audit
- Integration with 3PLs and carrier TMS systems

### Supply Chain Visibility
- Multi-tier supply chain visibility — see beyond Tier 1 suppliers
- Delivery risk scoring and predictive delay alerts
- Inventory buffers and demand signal sharing
- Event management for exceptions across the network

### Business Network for Sustainability
- Supplier ESG data collection and scoring
- Carbon footprint tracking through the supply chain (Scope 3)
- Supplier diversity tracking and reporting
- Conflict minerals and responsible sourcing compliance

## Business Value Propositions
- **Network effect**: Pre-connected to 6M+ suppliers — onboarding takes days, not months
- **Invoice automation**: 85–95% touchless invoice processing for network-connected suppliers
- **Supply chain resilience**: Early warning of supplier issues before they become shortages
- **Working capital**: Buyers optimize DPO; suppliers access early payment at favorable rates
- **Freight efficiency**: Automated carrier collaboration reduces manual communication by 70%
- **Compliance**: Built-in e-invoicing compliance for 70+ countries' tax and legal requirements
- **Carbon transparency**: Scope 3 emissions data from direct suppliers without surveys

## Pain Points Addressed
- Suppliers sending invoices via email, fax, or mail — manual data entry creates errors
- No visibility beyond Tier 1 suppliers — can't predict supply disruptions
- Supplier onboarding taking 3–6 months — slows down new product launches
- Freight collaboration via phone and email — no systematic exception management
- Inability to demonstrate sustainable supply chain to customers and investors
- Working capital tied up in long payment terms — no dynamic discounting program
- Lack of standardized supplier performance data
- Manual freight audit process — can't catch all billing errors
- Supply chain disruptions discovered late — no proactive risk monitoring

## Discovery Angles — What to Probe
- **Supplier connectivity**: Of your top 200 suppliers, how many are submitting invoices electronically today? What are the rest doing?
- **Invoice exceptions**: What percentage of your supplier invoices hit exceptions? What causes those exceptions?
- **Supplier risk monitoring**: How do you currently monitor your suppliers for financial health, compliance, or disruption risk?
- **Beyond Tier 1**: Do you have visibility into your sub-tier suppliers? Have you ever been surprised by a Tier 2 or Tier 3 failure?
- **Sustainability reporting**: Do you have Scope 3 emissions or supplier diversity reporting requirements? How do you collect that data today?
- **Early payment**: Do you offer early payment programs to your suppliers? Do suppliers ask for them?
- **Logistics collaboration**: How do your logistics planners interact with carriers today? How many are phone/email-based?
- **Onboarding time**: How long does it take to onboard a new supplier? What creates friction in that process?
- **Supply chain finance**: Do you have a supply chain financing program? What's preventing you from scaling it?
