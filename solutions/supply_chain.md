# SAP Supply Chain Management (SCM) — Solution Knowledge Base

## Solution Overview
SAP's supply chain portfolio within S/4HANA Cloud covers end-to-end supply chain execution: Extended Warehouse Management (EWM), Transportation Management (TM), and supply chain visibility. It integrates inbound and outbound logistics, warehouse operations, and carrier management on a single platform, giving companies real-time visibility from supplier to customer.

## Key Capabilities

### Extended Warehouse Management (EWM)
- Inbound and outbound warehouse processes with task management and work center optimization
- Slotting and yard management for optimal storage utilization
- Labor management and resource optimization
- Cross-docking, value-added services, and kitting
- RF and mobile device support for floor operations
- Integration with automation, conveyors, and autonomous mobile robots (AMRs)
- Dangerous goods handling and compliance

### Transportation Management (TM)
- Freight order management and carrier tendering (spot and contract rates)
- Load building and route optimization
- Carrier collaboration via SAP Business Network for Logistics
- Freight settlement and auditing — automated invoice verification against contracts
- Real-time shipment tracking and event management
- Intermodal and multi-leg planning
- Trade compliance and customs integration

### Supply Chain Visibility
- End-to-end order and shipment tracking
- Exception management and predictive alerts
- Integration with IoT sensors and external track-and-trace providers
- Delivery performance KPIs and carrier scorecards

### Inventory Management
- Real-time inventory across multiple locations in a single view
- Consignment and stock in transit visibility
- Expiry date management and batch tracking
- Serial number and lot traceability

## Business Value Propositions
- **Warehouse productivity**: 15–25% improvement in pick rates and labor efficiency through optimized task management
- **Transportation cost reduction**: 5–12% freight cost savings through load optimization, carrier competition, and contract compliance
- **On-time delivery improvement**: Reduce late deliveries by 20–40% with proactive exception management
- **Inventory reduction**: 10–20% inventory reduction through better visibility and demand signaling
- **Labor optimization**: Dynamic resource allocation based on workload forecasting
- **Carrier compliance**: Automated freight audit catches billing errors — typically 2–5% of freight invoices contain errors
- **Customer experience**: Real-time order status updates and proactive exception communication

## Pain Points Addressed
- Warehouse operations running on spreadsheets or legacy WMS disconnected from ERP
- Transportation managed in a TMS that doesn't talk to inventory or order management
- No real-time visibility into where shipments are — reactive rather than proactive
- Freight invoices paid without validation — carriers overbilling with no automated audit
- Manual carrier tendering via email and phone — no ability to leverage spot market
- Lack of end-to-end traceability for lot/serial-controlled products
- Multiple scanning, labeling, and packing errors causing customer deductions
- Difficulty scaling seasonal or peak warehouse operations
- No integration between warehouse and transportation — suboptimal load building

## Discovery Angles — What to Probe
- **Warehouse operations**: What WMS are you running today? Is it integrated with your ERP? What are your top 3 warehouse cost drivers?
- **Transportation spend**: How much do you spend on freight annually? How do you currently tender loads to carriers?
- **Visibility gaps**: At any given moment, how many shipments are "in the dark"? How do you handle exceptions?
- **Freight audit**: Do you audit all freight invoices? What percentage do you find have billing errors?
- **Customer service**: What's your current on-time, in-full (OTIF) performance? What are the main root causes when you miss it?
- **Labor efficiency**: How are you managing warehouse labor today? Do you have a labor management system?
- **Returns / reverse logistics**: How are you handling returns, refurbishments, and reverse logistics?
- **Automation plans**: Are you investing in warehouse automation (robotics, AMRs, conveyors)? Does your WMS support it?
- **Multi-site complexity**: How many DCs or fulfillment locations do you operate? How do you manage inventory allocation across them?

## Target Personas
- VP Supply Chain / Chief Supply Chain Officer: Wants end-to-end visibility and cost reduction
- VP Logistics / Logistics Director: Focused on transportation cost and on-time delivery
- Warehouse/DC Operations Manager: Wants better labor productivity and accuracy
- VP Operations: Wants to scale operations without proportional headcount increase
- CIO/IT: Wants to consolidate WMS and TMS onto ERP platform
- CFO: Wants to reduce freight costs and recover overbilled amounts
